The easiest plant resources to access were the proteins available in leaves and legumes, but these are difficult for primates like us to digest unless they are cooked.
In contrast, not only are animal foods (ants, termites, eggs) easy to digest, but they also provide high-quality proteins containing all the essential amino acids.
All in all, it is no surprise that our ancestors found a solution to their "protein problem", almost in the same way that chimpanzees on the savannah do today.
