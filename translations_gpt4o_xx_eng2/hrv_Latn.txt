When a small group of living beings (a small population) separates from the main population from which they came (for example, while crossing a mountain or a river, or if they move to a new island from which they cannot easily return), they will often find themselves in a different environment.

The original population has not changed at all; they still have to make the same adjustments as before.
Over time, as the new population begins to adapt to the new environment, it increasingly resembles the other population less.
Over time, after thousands or even millions of years, the two populations will differ so much in appearance that they can no longer be called the same species.
