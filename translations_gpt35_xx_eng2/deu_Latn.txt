This new environment has different resources and different competitors, so the new population needs different attributes or adjustments to be a strong competitor than what they previously needed.
The original population has not changed at all. It still needs the same adaptations as before.
Over time, the new population begins to adapt to its new environment, and then it becomes less and less similar to the other population.
After thousands or even millions of years, two populations then look so different that they can no longer be assigned to the same species.
This process is called speciation, which simply means the emergence of new species. Speciation is an inevitable consequence and a very important part of evolution.
