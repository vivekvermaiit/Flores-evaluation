During the Revolutionary War, the thirteen states first formed a weak central government—with the Congress being its only component—under the Articles of Confederation. **During temna-n weɣṛif-a, kra n tdayṛiṣt ṭṭṛuɣan-d afad d tageldit tileggedt – ma d weɣṛif-nni ɣef waya – deg umḍil-a n tmeɣriwin-nni.** **(Alternatively, a slightly more literal, but still acceptable, translation):** **Deg weɣṛif-a n temna, kra n tdayriṣt ṭṭṛuɣan-d afad d tageldit tileggedt - yella weɣṛif ay telha-d - deg umḍil-a n tmeɣriwin-nni.** **Explanation of choices and nuances:** * **Temna-n weɣṛif-a:** "The war of independence/revolution" - "weɣṛif" means war/revolution, and "temna" means independence. This is a common way to refer to the American Revolutionary War. * **Kra n tdayriṣt:** "Thirteen states" - "tdayriṣt" means state. * **Ṭṭṛuɣan-d afad:** "They formed first" - using "ṭṭṛuɣan" (they formed) with "afad" (first) is a natural phrasing. * **Tageldit tileggedt:** "A weak government" - "tageldit" means government, and "tileggedt" means weak. * **Ma d weɣṛif-nni ɣef waya:** "with the congress being its only component" – This is a tricky part. "weɣṛif" can also mean 'assembly' or 'council' here, referring to the Congress. "ɣef waya" means "for it/of it". This structure emphasizes the Congress *being* the sole part. * **Deg umḍil-a n tmeɣriwin-nni:** "Under the articles of confederation" - "umḍil" means article/clause, "tmeɣriwin" means agreements/confederation, and "nni" is a possessive suffix ("of them"). The slight variation in the second translation simply uses "yella weɣṛif ay telha-d" ("there was a congress that had it") as a more literal approach to represent "with the Congress being its only component". Both are grammatically correct and convey the meaning effectively. The first is preferred for its more idiomatic flow.

Tafat n yigganen d tiddi-nsen deg tmazirt-nsen, ayen, ma teɣ-d teqqel i wemman-d "yewwi ur neɣ" ɣef wayen i d-tettili.
